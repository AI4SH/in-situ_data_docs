    Salinity

    In the Netherhlands we used a soil penetrometer for determining what?

    which can be a simple two-probe device or a more complex four-electrode probe, passes an electrical current through the soil, and the resistance is measured

    Short method description

    Two or more electrodes are inserted into the soil to a specific depth. A voltage is applied across the electrodes. The resistance or conductivity of the soil to the current is measured. This measurement of electrical conductivity (EC) is used to estimate the total dissolved salts, which is a measure of soil salinity.
